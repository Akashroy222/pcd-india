Processing Community Day - Delhi

Jan 27th 2019 | Venue - Vasant Kunj

Processing community day India has been created by and for the local communities in India

Open Calls

PCD @ Delhi is an inclusive event that will bring together people of all ages to celebrate and explore art, code, and activism. The day-long event features workshops and sessions.... In addition to a full day of programming, we want to make space for anyone to share ideas and projects with the community. We will set up Show & Tell Stations, a Processing Community Cafe, and a Community Open Mic Session for participants to sign-up on the day of the event. The program will wrap up with an after party consists of performances, food, and drinks.


For any questions, email rintu.kutum@igib.in , natasha@timeblur.io , mike@timeblur.io

The Team

  • RINTU KUTUM is currently pursuing his PhD in Computational Biology at CSIR-Institute of Genomics & Integrative Biology, New Delhi. He did his B.Sc. in Biotechnology from Krupanidhi College, Bangalore University and M.Sc. in Biotechnology from KIIT-School of Biotechnology, KIIT University, Bhubaneswar. Now, he applies machine learning algorithms to solve problems in biology. Data visualisation is an integral part of data analysis and he uses constantly various basic graphics for his research.

  • NATASHA SINGH is a pioneer of generative art in India, using technology to demonstrate the combined power of mathematics and art. She graduated from NIFT Hyderabad in Product Design and Lifestyle and followed it up with a degree in Communication Design from Central Saint Martins School of Art and Design in London. She then started TimeBlur, a digital experience studio, in 2015. Natasha has always done things differently and is passionate about promoting the ancient practices of India like yoga with the help of cutting edge technology. She works with New-Media technologies like Human Brain Interfaces, Human Computer Interaction, Computer Vision, and Motion Tracking. She has set sail on an adventure to explore the world beyond traditional design with machines and code to Digitally conserve the Ancient practices of India and Digitally transform the world.

  • MIKE CJ is a technocrat from Bangalore, with a graduate degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering. He works primarily with electronic hardware, Signal Processing and computer graphics, which is applied in interdisciplinary fields such as Music, Software arts and Data Visualization. He loves being in the complexity of computer algorithms to generate beautiful artforms. The passion for making art lead to starting Timeblur Studio, in 2015 along with Natasha Singh and is currently working as a creative developer where he deeply works on the technical side of things.
